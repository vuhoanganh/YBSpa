WHAT ARE EYELASH EXTENSIONS?

1. They are single strands of synthetic eyelashes, available in many fiber types such as silk or mink, that are curved to replicate a natural lash. They are glued to your upper line so they look natural and part of your real lashes. How safe are they?

2. Individual lashes are dipped into a medical-grade adhesive and bonded to your natural eyelash so they are 100% safe. How comfortable do they feel?

3. They are soft on lashes and allow great flexibility and comfort. If you are extra sensitive, choose silk or mink lashes over synthetic hair ones. How long do last?

4. They last up to 6 weeks and you can refill after the initial full-lash application. How much do they cost?

5. They typically cost from $50-$100 but at Young & Beautiful Salon & Spa, our price ranges from $50-$60, depending on thickness and the types of lashes you want. They take about an hour to apply.

WHAT IS SHELLAC?

1. It’s a popular hybrids gel/polish manicure that’s cured with UV or LED light to harden the polish. How is Shellac different from fake nails?

2. Fake nails require a drill for application while Shellac is much more gentle on natural nails. How long does Shellac last?

3. It lasts for 14 days, and with good maintenance, can last up to a month. Can I remove the Shellac at home?

4. You should not attempt to remove it yourself because you might damage the top layer of your nails. The salon has extra strength acetone can loosen the shellac in a short time. What can I do to protect my hands while the Shellac is being cured under UV or LED light?

5. The curing process is very quick and takes about less than 5 minutes. If you are uncomfortable exposing your skin under the UV light, you can rub sunblock on your hands prior to the curing treatment.

5 HAIR CARE NIGHTMARES
We all are guilty of these bad hair practices, but let’s curb them once and for all.

1. Drying sopping wet hair: This will result in longer drying hair, putting your hair under more harmful heat. Furthermore, when you use round brush on sopping wet hair, the friction frays the cuticle which will lead to flat and damaged hair. The best practice is to towel dry, then leave it to air dry for 15-20 minutes until nearly dried, then use your blow dryer to style.

2. Washing away color: Since 75% or more women color their hair, this mistake is a costly one. Wait at least 48 hours to wash your hair after a new color job, and do use specially formulated shampoo and conditioner, those that help retain colors, to keep your locks vibrant longer.

3. Using hairspray before styling: Under no circumstances should you do this. Hairspray with a high content of alcohol will burn your hair beyond repair. Always use hairspray after styling with heat products.

4. Washing hair everyday: You have heard this advice many times, that washing hair everyday will strip away your hair’s natural oil, but many of us find it hard to go to sleep with a dirty head. Here is your solution: you can wash it everyday but don’t use shampoo; just wash with water and apply a light volumizing conditioner. Be sure to rinse carefully to avoid conditioner residue.

5. Tying hair too tight: Whether it is ponytails, braids or hair extensions, the pressure puts tension on your hair and will cause damage to the cuticle and can lead to balding. Avoid using hair extensions or putting your hair up for prolong periods. Embrace diversity and give your hair a good rest in between.
